ATTiny85 Prototyping Board

A custom PCB designed and fabricated to provide a clean, reusable development platform for the ATTiny85 microcontroller — with I/O breakout headers, onboard LED, reset button, and proper decoupling.

Overview

The ATTiny85 is a capable 8-pin AVR microcontroller — small, cheap, and well-suited for embedded projects that don't need the overhead of a full Arduino. The problem is that prototyping with it on a breadboard gets messy fast: the DIP-8 footprint is easy to displace, power rails are fragile, and there's no clean way to interface external peripherals without things turning into a tangle of jumper wires.

This board solves that by giving the ATTiny85 a proper home. It's a simple but deliberate PCB: the chip plugs into a DIP-8 socket, power is decoupled with 100nF capacitors, I/O pins are broken out to female headers on both sides, and a pushbutton and LED are included onboard for basic interaction without any external components.

V1 — Designed in KiCad

The schematic and PCB layout were done in KiCad. The board was fabricated externally and hand-soldered. This is a first version — functional and intentionally minimal, with the goal of establishing a reliable base for ATTiny85 firmware development and iteration.

Design & Schematic

The circuit is deliberately simple. The ATTiny85 sits at the center with its 8 pins broken out through two 4-pin female headers — giving clean access to all I/O, power, and ground pins without needing a breadboard. A 100nF decoupling capacitor on each power supply pin keeps the supply stable during switching transients.

The pushbutton is wired to the reset line, allowing the chip to be restarted without removing it from the socket. The onboard LED with a current-limiting resistor gives a useful indicator output that's immediately available in firmware without wiring anything externally.

Board Features

What's on the board

  • DIP-8 socket — ATTiny85 plugs in and out without soldering; easy to swap chips during development
  • Dual 4-pin I/O headers — all ATTiny85 pins broken out to female headers for clean peripheral attachment
  • Onboard LED + resistor — immediately usable output pin for debugging, status indication, or just blinking
  • Reset pushbutton — restart the chip without removing it or cycling power
  • 100nF decoupling caps — stable power supply during I/O switching; good practice for any AVR design
  • +5V powered — compatible with USB power or standard bench supply

The design prioritizes practicality: everything you'd want for iterating on ATTiny85 firmware is on the board, and nothing you wouldn't. It's sized to be compact without being fiddly to work with by hand.

Fabrication & Assembly

The board was sent out for fabrication and returned as bare PCBs. Components were hand-soldered — through-hole throughout, which keeps assembly straightforward and makes rework easy if a connection needs to be redone.

Components were hand-soldered in assembly order: decoupling capacitors first, then the DIP-8 socket, I/O headers, LED, and resistor. The pushbutton footprint is on the board but not populated in V1 — the reset line is accessible via the breakout header instead.
