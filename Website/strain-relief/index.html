Parametric Strain Relief

3D Printable Cable Grommet · Fully Customizable

A parametric strain relief (cable grommet) sized to any cable and panel. It consists of a flat base ring that sits on the outer face of an enclosure, and a flared body that passes through the panel hole and grips the cable at the tip. Switch between circular, square, and helical profiles using the toggle in the panel. Tune every dimension with the sliders and download a print-ready STL.

Size the cord hole to your cable's outer diameter for a firm grip, or add 0.5 mm for a softer press fit. The base inner diameter should match your panel hole. Wall thickness controls stiffness: thinner walls flex more and grip better; thicker walls are more rigid and durable.

Design Notes

Print Settings

Print with the flat base face down (circular and square profiles), no supports needed. Use 3-4 perimeters and 20-40% infill. PETG is ideal, as it has enough flex to grip the cable without cracking. ABS works for heat-exposed installations. PLA is fine for light-duty indoor use.

Sizing Guide

  • Cord hole: match to your cable OD for a firm grip, or add 0.5 mm for a press fit, or add 1-2 mm if you plan to seal with a zip tie or silicone.
  • Base inner diameter: should equal your panel hole diameter exactly (or +0.2 mm for a slip fit).
  • Base outer diameter: needs to be large enough to catch the panel face, at least 5 mm larger than the panel hole on each side.
  • Wall thickness: 2-3 mm is typical. Go thinner for cables under 6 mm OD; thicker for cables over 15 mm OD or rough installation environments.

How the Geometry Is Generated

Circular and square profiles are analytic triangle meshes built directly in Three.js without CSG or mesh booleans. Each face's outward normals are derived analytically from the frustum's slant angle, giving correct shading on tapered surfaces. The helical profile sweeps a rectangular cross-section along a parametric helix using a stable radial/vertical frame (no Frenet twist), then merges base and top collars. All three profiles export as binary STL with no server round-trip.
