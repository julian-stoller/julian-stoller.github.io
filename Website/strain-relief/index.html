Parametric Strain Relief

3D Printable Cable Grommet · Fully Customizable

A parametric strain relief (cable grommet) sized to any cable and panel. It consists of a flat base ring that sits on the outer face of an enclosure, and a flared conical body that passes through the panel hole and grips the cable with a tight cord opening at the tip. Tune every dimension with the sliders and download a print-ready STL.

Size the cord hole to your cable's outer diameter for a firm grip, or add 0.5 mm for a softer press fit. The base inner diameter should match your panel hole. Wall thickness controls stiffness — thinner walls flex more and grip better; thicker walls are more rigid and durable.

Design Notes

Print Settings

Print with the flat base face down, no supports needed. Use 3–4 perimeters and 20–40% infill. PETG is ideal — it has enough flex to grip the cable without cracking. ABS works for heat-exposed installations. PLA is fine for light-duty indoor use.

Sizing Guide

  • Cord hole: match to your cable OD for a firm grip, or add 0.5 mm for a press fit, or add 1–2 mm if you plan to seal with a zip tie or silicone.
  • Base inner diameter: should equal your panel hole diameter exactly (or +0.2 mm for a slip fit).
  • Base outer diameter: needs to be large enough to catch the panel face — at least 5 mm larger than the panel hole on each side.
  • Wall thickness: 2–3 mm is typical. Go thinner for cables under 6 mm OD; thicker for cables over 15 mm OD or rough installation environments.

How the Geometry Is Generated

The shape is a hollow frustum (truncated cone) sitting on a flat ring, both generated as analytic triangle meshes directly in Three.js — no CSG, no mesh booleans. Each face's outward normals are computed from the frustum's slant angle, giving correct shading on the tapered surface. The binary STL download writes directly from the GPU-ready buffer without any conversion step.
