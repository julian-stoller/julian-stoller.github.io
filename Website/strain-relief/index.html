Parametric Strain Relief

3D Printable Cable Grommet · Fully Customizable

A parametric strain relief (also called a cable grommet) is a simple but surprisingly useful part: it grips a cable where it passes through a panel or enclosure, preventing tension from reaching a solder joint or connector inside. This design is fully parametric — drag the sliders to size it for any cable and panel thickness, then download a print-ready STL.

Two mounting styles are supported. Panel-Mount adds a wide flange that rests on the outer face of the panel; the body passes through a drilled hole. Inline removes the flange for use as a pure grip sleeve or where the hole edges provide retention. Longitudinal fins around the body improve hand-grip when installing and increase radial friction against the hole wall.

Design Notes

Print Settings

Print with 3–4 perimeters and 30–40% infill in PETG or ABS for good flex-resistance. The flange face benefits from a brim to prevent warping on heated-bed printers. No supports are needed for most orientations — print with the flat flange face down.

Sizing Guide

  • Hole diameter: match to your cable OD, or add 0.3–0.5 mm for a press fit, or add 1–1.5 mm for a slip fit you can tighten with a zip tie.
  • Body diameter: should be 2–4 mm larger than the panel hole for the fins to bite. Scale fins down if clearance is tight.
  • Flange diameter: at least 4 mm larger than the panel hole on each side so the flange catches cleanly.
  • Body length: should exceed panel thickness by at least 10 mm to provide enough grip length for the cable.

Parametric Design Approach

The geometry is generated live in the browser using Three.js BufferGeometry. Each slider update rebuilds the mesh from scratch using analytic geometry — no CSG library is needed because the hollow-cylinder and fin shapes are generated directly as indexed triangle meshes. The STL export writes a binary STL file entirely in JavaScript without any server round-trip.
