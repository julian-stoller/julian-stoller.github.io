Overview

The Architectural Robotics Lab (ARL) at Cornell University focuses on the intersection of robotics, human-robot interaction (HRI), and architecture. As a member of the lab, I've contributed to research in human-robot interaction and autonomous robotic systems.

Contributions to User Studies in Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)

I have participated in and contributed to user studies exploring how humans interact with robotic systems. These studies help inform better robot design and control strategies for real-world applications.

Contributions to S.O.R.T. (Autonomous Robot System)

  • Novel Suction Cup Design: Engineered a custom suction cup mechanism for improved adherence and reliability on varied surfaces.
  • Magnetic Wheel vs. Magnetic Tracks Analysis: Comparative analysis of propulsion methods, evaluating trade-offs in cost, complexity, performance, and manufacturability.
  • Kinematics Simulations in MATLAB: Developed MATLAB simulations to model and optimize robot kinematics for autonomous navigation and manipulation tasks.
