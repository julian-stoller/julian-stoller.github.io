High-Performance Lightweight Gear for Electric Vehicles

MAE 4450 Cornell University Spring 2026 Generative Design Topology Optimization FEA / Ansys Additive Manufacturing Coursework

Overview

For Project #2 in MAE 4450 (Process Physics, Materials, Properties, and Post Processing), I used Fusion 360 generative design to minimize the mass of a 12-tooth gear for an experimental electric vehicle while satisfying a set of explicit and implicit design constraints.

The gear had to withstand a 1000 N mid-stall load on a single tooth at a factor of safety of 1.2. To keep every tooth equally supported and to remove any dependence on mounting orientation, I set up the study with a fixed constraint on the bore, 12 equi-angular symmetry planes through the center axis, and a 13th mid-plane symmetry plane. The tooth contour and bore surface were preserved while the material between them was released for topology optimization.

The study was run as a proxy for powder bed fusion (PBF) with no manufacturing constraints, comparing two candidate materials: Aluminum AlSi10Mg and Titanium Ti-6Al-4V. The optimized meshes were then re-imported into Ansys for an independent stress check.

Results

Both converged geometries satisfied the 1.2 FOS requirement but presented meaningful trade-offs between mass, stiffness, and safety margin:

Material Mass Max Displacement Min FOS
Aluminum AlSi10Mg 0.093 kg 0.326 mm 2.288
Titanium Ti-6Al-4V 0.154 kg 0.206 mm 8.421

I recommended moving forward with the aluminum part for its lower mass, which improves vehicle range and reduces the energy needed to spin the gear at high speed, while noting that fatigue, backlash, and displacement limits would need to be watched during cycle testing. The Ansys re-analysis showed a stress singularity at a filleted corner, but the effective peak stresses (89.12 MPa aluminum, 88.51 MPa titanium) were consistent with the generative results. An addendum explored an alternative preserve geometry that traded a small mass increase (0.1 kg) for the lowest displacement of any generated design (0.144 mm).
