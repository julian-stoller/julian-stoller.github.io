Overview

A collection of smaller CAD and fabrication projects that explore various design challenges, manufacturing techniques, and problem-solving approaches. While individually focused, these projects collectively showcase design versatility and practical fabrication experience.

Projects

Laptop Sticker Case for External SSD

A custom-designed protective enclosure for an external solid-state drive, optimized for portability and adhesion to laptop surfaces. Focused on ergonomic design and minimal aesthetic impact while maintaining functionality and protection.

Replacement Piece for Thrifted Radio Shack Calipers

Reverse-engineered and redesigned a missing component for vintage measurement calipers. This project required precise dimensioning based on complementary parts and a deep understanding of mechanical tolerances and assembly interfaces.

3D Printed Tessellating Calendar Stamps

A set of parametrically designed stamps that tessellate for calendar date marking. Combines mathematical design, 3D printing optimization, and user-centered design for a functional craft tool.

Design Approach

  • Reverse Engineering: Understanding and reproducing designs from physical components and specifications
  • Parametric Design: Building flexibility into designs for quick iteration and customization
  • 3D Printing Optimization: Designing specifically for additive manufacturing constraints and capabilities
  • Functional Problem-Solving: Creating practical solutions to real-world design challenges
  • Aesthetic Consideration: Balancing form and function in consumer-facing products

Skills Demonstrated

  • CAD modeling (SOLIDWORKS, Fusion 360)
  • Design for 3D printing (FDM)
  • Precision dimensioning and tolerancing
  • Reverse engineering techniques
  • Parametric design principles
  • User experience design
  • Fabrication and post-processing
