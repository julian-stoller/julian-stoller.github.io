Big Red Hacks 2025

Customized Kendama with Additive Manufacturing, Parametric CAD, and Interchangeable Parts

In September 2025, I decided to participate in Big Red Hacks, a hackathon taking place on campus from 09/19 to 09/21. As a Mechanical Engineering student, I knew hackathons were more of a software development thing, but I wanted to participate in an in-person hackathon before graduating.

The theme was Entertainment, and I designed what I called a "Customized Kendama with AM, Parametric CAD, and Interchangeable Parts." My central question was: How can I add value to a rather simple toy by strategically applying design principles I hold in high regard?

üîß Interchangeable Parts

A kendama with interchangeable parts could grow with a user as they progress from beginner to expert:

  • Beginners can replace the 'cup' with larger ones to make learning easier, and can swap out the string for different materials or lengths.
  • Experts can make using a kendama harder by installing smaller cups or modifying other features (handle length, weight distribution, cup shape, etc.) to increase the challenge.
  • Customization: Users can personalize their kendamas by swapping parts with different colors or custom branding.

üñ®Ô∏è 3D Printed Manufacturing

Designing this kendama to be 3D printable opens accessibility to anyone with access to a printer:

  • Design for Manufacturability (DFM): By designing parts to be difficult to print incorrectly, the overall quality matches that of traditional wooden kendamas, even for users without technical printing knowledge.
  • Open Accessibility: Publishing geometries on sites like Thingiverse enables user-in-the-loop revisions and allows a greater level of customization.
  • Barrier to Entry: 3D printing democratizes access to this toy, removing cost and manufacturing barriers.

üìê Parametric CAD

A parametric CAD approach lets anyone customize without needing to learn CAD software:

  • Easy Editing: Parameters like cup diameter, handle length, and weight distribution are immediately visible and simple to adjust.
  • Lower Technical Barrier: Users don't need deep CAD knowledge to customize their kendama‚Äîjust modify slider values or parameters in a spreadsheet.
  • Scalability: The same base design can be adapted for a wide range of skill levels and preferences.

With all of this in mind, I designed a fully 3D-printable kendama with interchangeable parts where relevant and parametric dimensions easily customizable in CAD software.
